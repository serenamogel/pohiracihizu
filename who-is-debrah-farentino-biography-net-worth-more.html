Debrah Farentino (born Deborah Mullowney) is an actress, producer, and journalist from the United States. From 1982 to 1987, she starred in the CBS daytime soap opera Capitol before transitioning to prime time with a female main role in the ABC comedic drama series Hooperman (1987-88).

Who is Debrah Farentino?

Deborah Mullowney was born in Lucas Valley, California, and worked as a model before becoming an actress.

Farentino starred in a number of dramatic shows in the 1990s, including Equal Justice (1990-91), Earth 2 (1994-95), EZ Streets (1996-97), and Get Real (1999-2000). Her other prominent roles include the 1993 comedy Son of the Pink Panther, the 1999 miniseries Storm of the Century, and the Syfy comedy-drama Eureka (2006-2012).

What is Debrah Farentino’s profession?

Farentino’s acting career began in 1982 when she was hired in the television series Capitol. She has appeared in almost fifty films and television shows since then, including Hooperman (1987-1989) and Son of the Pink Panther (1993). Her most well-known role was as Devon Adair in NBC’s SciFi series Earth 2, where she was the first female commander featured in a science fiction production.

She has appeared in numerous television shows over the course of four decades, including NYPD Blue (1994), the revival of The Outer Limits (1996), JAG (2002), CSI: Miami (2003), and Hawaii Five-0 (2011). Her most recent performances were Isabelle Matia-Paris in ABC’s Wildfire and Beverly Barlowe in Syfy Channel’s Eureka.

Farentino has created PBS/WXEL specials, including “Saving America’s Heroes,” which received a Suncoast Emmy nomination. She has also appeared on CBS News as a special correspondent covering Guardian Angel troops and has embedded with USAF Special Forces rescue units in Afghanistan on many occasions.

While pregnant with her daughter Sophie, she was named one of People Magazine’s “50 Most Beautiful People” in 1995.
