Lisa | Music & Endorsements

Music

Lisa had an interest in K-pop idols since she was very young. She admired artists such as 2NE1 and BigBang and also wished to someday follow a similar path.

Lisa joined the girls’ group Blackpink in 2016. The group released their debut album entitled “Blackpink In Your Area,” in 2018, which reached number 9 in Japan.

The same year their EP “Square Up” was released, which reached number 1 in Korea.  Similarly, in 2019 the group released another EP titled “Kill This Love,” which reached number 1 on the US World Chart and 33 in Korea.

Moreover, the group’s self-titled album “Blackpink” reached number 1 in Japan.

In 2021 Lisa released her debut solo album LALISA smashing several records in K-pop history.

Aside from that, Lisa appeared in episodes of the television series Real Man 300 and Lisa TV.

Lisa | Career

Lisa was born Pranpriya Manobal in Buriram Province, Thailand, on March 27, 1997.

She changed her name to Lalisa at the suggestion of a fortune teller in order to bring success.

Lisa started dancing when she was four and also competed in dance competitions throughout her childhood.

She joined an eleven-member dance crew named We Zaa Cool.

Lisa auditioned for South Korean record label YG Entertainment in Thailand in 2010; she was 13 years old at the time.

Interestingly, there were 4,000 applicants and guess what Lisa was the only individual to qualify.

She was selected, and in 2011, she moved to South Korea to begin her formal training. The training lasted five years.
